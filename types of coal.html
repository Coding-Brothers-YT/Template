Types of Coal

There are majorly 5 types of coal depending upon the amount of carbon, vapour, volatile matter, chemical content and mositure. They are:


  1. 1. Peat, a precursor of coal
  2. 2. Lignite, or brown coal, the lowest rank of coal, most harmful to health, used almost exclusively as fuel for electric power generation
  3. 3. Sub-bituminous coal, whose properties range between those of lignite and those of bituminous coal, is used primarily as fuel for steam-electric power generation
  4. 4. Bituminous coal, a dense sedimentary rock, usually black, but sometimes dark brown, often with well-defined bands of bright and dull material. It is used primarily as fuel in steam-electric power generation and to make coke. Known as steam coal in the UK, and historically used to raise steam in steam locomotives and ships
  5. 5. Anthracite, the highest rank of coal is a harder, glossy black coal used primarily for residential and commercial space heating
